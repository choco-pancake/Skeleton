TIPS FOR CARING YOUR FELINE FRIEND
Every cat is an individual with unique needs and preferences. Pay attention to your
cat's behavior and adjust their care routine accordingly. With love, attention, and proper
care, you can ensure a long and happy life for your feline companion.

Food and Water:
Choose a diet specifically formulated for cats, considering their age and activity level.
Avoid feeding them human food, as it can be harmful. Always provide fresh, clean water,
ideally in multiple locations around the house and establish a regular feeding schedule to
prevent overeating and maintain their weight.

Grooming:
Brush your cat regularly, especially if they have long hair, to remove loose fur and
prevent mats. Cats are generally good at grooming themselves, but you may need to bathe
them occasionally and Trim their nails every few weeks to prevent scratching on furniture
and people.

Play and Enrichment:
Play with your cat regularly using toys like feathers, laser pointers, and catnip mice.
This provides mental and physical stimulation. Provide scratching posts to satisfy their
natural scratching instincts. Offer puzzle toys and treat dispensers to keep them entertained
and mentally engaged.

Veterinary Care:
Keep your cat up-to-date on their vaccinations to protect them from diseases.
Administer flea and tick preventatives as recommended by your veterinarian. Schedule
annual checkups to ensure they are healthy.


Safety:
Keep toxic houseplants out of reach, as they can be harmful to cats. Store
medications safely and securely, as cats can be curious and may ingest them. Ensure windows
are secured or covered to prevent falls.
